The Birth of a Viral Drink

"While at a local market, the man noticed tapioca balls - a traditional Taiwanese snack remembered from his childhood. He decided to add them to the green tea he used to drink in the evenings. And so, according to this version of events, bubble tea was born." Bubble tea, also known as boba, didn't become as popular as it is today overnight. Although milk tea, the og bubble tea was already widely drank in asian countries, it was in the 2010's with the growing cafe culture and its aesthetics posted on social media when it really started taking off

There are various "types" of boba:

    Milk teas
    Brown Sugar Milk - the one that popularized bubble tea
    Fruit teas

And of course theres plenty of toppings as well! Here are just a few

    Tapioca pearls
    Sago
    Milk Foams
