So.. what is boba? Whats the hype?

Boba is really just starch balls. What makes them so fun to eat is it's chewy texture, and the refreshing drink that accompanies it. Boba—the drink in its entirety—originates from Taiwan, though its disputed which city and specific shop it actually started from. Originally, boba pearls were used in shaved ice desserts and paired with syrups, beans, and delectably chewy rice balls. Milk tea was also consumed regularly and thankfully, someone decided to merge the two, thus creating the genius, beloved drink we now have today. Boba culture made its way to America through Taiwanese neighborhoods and blossomed near college campuses and high schools, where students would gather for study groups. Most boba shops, even now, are open late and offer affordable snacks and drinks, which make them the perfect stop for late-night hang outs and studying.

Mini Folklore

"While at a local market, the man noticed tapioca balls - a traditional Taiwanese snack remembered from his childhood. He decided to add them to the green tea he used to drink in the evenings. And so, according to this version of events, bubble tea was born." Bubble tea, also known as boba, didn't become as popular as it is today overnight. Although milk tea, the og bubble tea was already widely drank in asian countries, it was in the 2010's with the growing cafe culture and its aesthetics posted on social media when it really started taking off.
